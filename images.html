Step 1

First Iteration — 7 Models

The goal was simple: find which AI image generator handles on-image text best. One prompt, seven models, no post-processing. The scene — a person sitting alone in an empty room — was chosen to test both visual storytelling and typographic clarity simultaneously.

Prompt used across all models
A lonely young adult sitting on the floor in the corner of a large empty room, knees pulled to chest. Blurred silhouettes of people pass outside a glass wall, ignoring the character. Mood: emotional isolation, feeling unseen. TEXT (must be large, clean, high contrast, fully readable, centered top-to-middle layout): This will hurt, are you ready? You feel invisible. Like you could disappear and no one would even notice. It's not just loneliness. It's being alone in a crowd. Alone in your feelings. Alone in your opinions. Maybe you learned early on that no one was really listening. You had to handle it all yourself. And now you're still waiting for someone to actually see you. There is an app called Voiced, where you can talk things through and actually reflect. It's private, judgment-free, and the AI actually helps you spot the patterns, makes you reflect. Basically a journal that writes back. This is probably the best tool for self-reflection on the market. Tap on the screen if you are ready to try. Typography: bold headline, clean sans-serif, strong hierarchy, paragraph readable. Leave visual in lower 30–40% of frame. No logos, no UI, no watermark.

💡
Verdict: Both NanoBanana and GPT handle text reliably — but GPT thinks in layouts

NanoBanana Pro and NanoBanana 2 produce consistently clean, legible text with correct hierarchy across all prompts. GPT-4o Image matches them on text quality but goes further — it experiments with visual composition, text positioning, and typographic rhythm in ways the NanoBanana models don't attempt. If consistency is the priority, choose NanoBanana. If creative layout variety matters, GPT wins.

Step 3

Resize Test — 4×5 → 9×16

UGC creatives live in a 9:16 world, but generating directly in 9:16 often breaks layout. The more reliable pipeline: generate at 4:5, then expand to 9:16 using an outpainting tool. We tested three models plus Freepik's built-in expand tool to see which handles the format shift without distorting the subject or losing text legibility.

Freepik Expand — clear winner
Best
Freepik Expand

Freepik's native Expand tool outperforms all three AI models on the outpainting task. It preserves the original composition cleanly, adds contextually coherent backgrounds, and most importantly — doesn't touch the text area. The on-image copy stays pixel-perfect while the frame extends naturally.

The AI models (even NanoBanana Pro) hallucinated new elements near the edges, distorted background consistency, and in GPT's case altered lighting conditions in the expanded zones.

✅
Final pipeline: Generate at 4×5 → Expand with Freepik → Export as 9×16

Freepik Expand is the only tool that reliably handles the aspect ratio shift without introducing artifacts or distorting the subject. For any UGC static creative workflow, this is now the recommended format adaptation step.
