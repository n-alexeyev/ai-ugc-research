Step 2 — Avatar Generation

Four Generators Tested

Portrait generation is the bridge between LLM output and video generation. We tested four tools head-to-head.

NanoBanana 2

Fast, highly consistent output. Excellent as an intermediate step for first-frame generation. Best cost-to-quality ratio tested.

⭐ Winner

NanoBanana Pro

Higher quality than NB2 but noticeably slower and more expensive. Not worth the overhead for intermediate steps.

Recraft v4 Pro

Most photorealistic portrait quality of all tested. Produces the highest-fidelity avatar images. Best choice for final visual reference.

⭐ Winner

Grok

Experimental quality results. Limited consistency across generations. Not suitable for production pipeline use.

Step 3 — Architecture

The Full Workflow

3 video models × 2 avatar generators × 3 LLMs = 18 generation branches tested in parallel.

Approach 1

Full-Length Prompt

Write one 30-second prompt → split into two 15-sec clips → generate in Kling 3 Omni and Seedance 2.

1
Write one 30-sec prompt
Split into 2 × 15-sec segments for generation
2
Generate in Kling 3 Omni / Seedance 2
Both models tested with all 3 LLM prompt variants
3
Stitch clips in Freepik
Combine segments into final 30-sec video
⚠️ Problem found: Model hallucinations — scenes were skipped or distorted. Regenerating the full video is expensive, especially in Seedance (5× Kling cost). Any single bad scene requires regenerating the entire clip.

LLMs as Prompt Writers: Verdict

Claude

Best for detailed, structured scene breakdowns. Rich visual specificity — ideal when prompt quality is the critical variable.

Gemini

Best balance of brevity and completeness. Clean numbered outputs are easy to edit and reuse across models.

ChatGPT

Most creative phrasing and cinematographic vocabulary. Good for expressive, stylized UGC content.

All three LLMs handled the task well. Differences are stylistic — the real variable is how you craft the prompt, not which model you use.
